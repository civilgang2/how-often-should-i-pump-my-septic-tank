Introduction of Septic Tank Be Pumped

In nature, a septic tank should be pumped in every 3 to 5 years but in the normal day-to-day life in a city life it does not pump at that regular interval; so, the tank will choke. Actually, the place, outside of the city, does not have a sewer connection; so, they use a septic tank for the same purpose.

Generally, a septic tank is an eco-friendly system because it does not harm nature, it is safe and it uses a natural way to decompose the wastes.

Basically, with proper care and maintenance, a septic tank will last many years without any trouble but if there is no maintenance, the owner may face some serious difficulties with that septic tank.

In the septic tank the waste enters the septic tank, the solid wastes will separate and the liquid wastes and scum will be separated. The solid wastes are heavy so they will settle down and the bacteria & microorganisms will dissolve that, scum is made with fat, oil, grease so they are very lightweight and they will float and secure the top portion.

The watery parts are in the middle portion and will, move out from the tank by a perforated underground pipe and releases to the ground. Then ground act as a filtration system and purifies the water and then it goes to the groundwater system.

Sometimes, the waste materials are too many amounts and it reduces the effectiveness of those bacteria, at that time they can not decompose the sludge, and at that time the wastewater will overflow to the ground a hazardous situation is created. So, sort out that problem you need to pump the septic tank at an interval of 3 to 4 years.

How to Identify the Septic Tank Is Full?

At first, you need to remove the upper lid of the septic tank, and this time you need to be more cautious about the breakage of the lid. If the lid will break then it will be very harmful to us because it contains 4.5 feet of underground water and toxic gases like methane.

So, if a man or an animal will fall into the septic tank it will be very dangerous. After that, you need to check out the scum trap to test the thickness of the scum layer. Generally, when the scum layer is above 6 inches thick, then you need to remove the scum layer.

Then you need to measure the sludge amount of the septic tank. To measure the sludge level you can buy a sludge stick from the market or you can prepare a sludge measuring stick.

The stick should be seven feet long and one side Velcro strip will be attached, that portion will go inside the septic tank to measure the sludge thickness layer.

After that you should gently put the stick inside the sludge, you should not stop inserting the stick until you can find a hard bottom. Then remove the stick and measure the height of the sludge.

So, you can easily calculate the height of the septic tank sludge and you need to pump the sludge if the height of it reaches to 1 foot or 12 inches.

You can do this work by your own ability but if you hire a professional company for this work, they can do it more preciously and more precautions will be maintained.

Procedure of Septic Tank Be Pumped

There are a few steps that are followed to pump a septic tank, some rules are regulations are also maintained when this operation is done. Those are in the following below-

Step 1. Determine the Size of the Septic Tank:

Generally, the size of a septic tank is near about 1000 to 2000 gallons. In case, if you can not determine the size of your septic tank a professional company can do this job for you.

The size of the septic tank is very much essential because without measuring the septic tank you can not determine the volume of that septic tank, and if you can not determine the septic tank then you can not calculate how much pumping operation is needed for your septic tank.

For example, a septic tank of 1000 gallons should be pumped within 2.6 years, a 1500 gallon tank should be pumped for 4.2 years and for 2000 gallons tank is recommended for 5 years.

Step 2. To Determine the Household Members:

Generally, the size of the household member is proportional to the size of the septic tank. If the number of members is more then the size of the septic tank should be more. Contact those members to get a better idea about that.

Naturally, people produce more waste materials rather than measured ones, so, you need to consider that amount of waste. In households, there are huge use of soaps, chemicals, detergents, etc. But it will also affect the time factors of the septic tank.

Step 3. Considering the Other Wastes Produced by Dishwashing, Laundry, Shower, Etc:

We need to remember that the amount of waste is also coming from those places like laundry, washing dishes, etc. This type produces a lot of waste.

We all suggest that you need to use the washing machine throughout the week but do not put too much pressure in one day, that will create problems by discharging too much water and the septic tank will be overflowed. That is very problematic.

You need to use biodegradable soaps and detergents that will help the bacteria, use an energy-efficient washing machine that produces less amount of water that will reduce the chances of overflow, use a filter that can resist the synthetic fibers because the synthetic fibers will slow down the digestion rate of bacteria.

If someone is using a kitchen sink then it is strictly recommended that never use a garbage disposal, it will not reduce the waste but it will increase the waste by 50 percent and that creates a problematic situation for septic tank users. Reduce the usage of shower water because this will tremendously increase the volume of water.

Step 4. Pumping Interval of Septic Tank

Pumping of septic tank is not always costly but sometimes it’s costly when you need to dig in deep or proceeding repair works in a deep portion and as well as in maintenance too.

There are a few rules of pumping which would be maintained all over India. Thus septic tank of 1000 gallons should be pumped within 2.6 years, a 1500 gallon tank should be pumped for 4.2 years, and a 2000 gallons tank is recommended for 5 years. So, these are the intervals of a pumped interval of a septic tank and this is the process that how a septic tank be pumped.
